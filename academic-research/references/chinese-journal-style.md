# Chinese Journal Style

## Use Case

Use this profile for Chinese CSSCI-style writing, Chinese core-journal-style articles, article submission drafts, and other Chinese scholarly prose that needs to be tighter, sharper, and more publication-oriented.

## Overall Character

This style should feel:

- compact
- focused
- argument-driven
- selective rather than exhaustive
- publication-oriented

It should read as if every paragraph earns its place.

## Structural Preference

Prefer writing that:

- states the problem quickly
- limits background to what is necessary for the argument
- enters the literature dialogue early
- highlights the paper's positioning and marginal contribution sooner
- compresses transitions and removes explanatory detours

Do not reproduce thesis-style expansion in journal-style prose.

## Tone

The tone should be:

- formal
- crisp
- analytically dense
- restrained but confident

Each sentence should advance the argument, not merely decorate it.

## Introduction Style

In Chinese journal-style writing, introductions usually work best when they:

1. state the problem fast
2. show why the problem matters to the field
3. identify the live debate or unresolved issue
4. position the paper against prior work
5. state the contribution and paper structure succinctly

Do not spend too long on general background that most field readers already know.

## Section-Level Sentence Bank

Use sentence shapes like these when drafting journal-style Chinese prose. Adapt them into natural Chinese rather than translating them literally.

### Opening Move

- "A persistent problem in the literature on ... is ..."
- "Current discussion of ... has produced important insights, but ..."
- "The key difficulty is not whether ..., but how ..."

### Positioning Against Prior Work

- "Most existing studies explain ... from the perspective of ..., whereas this paper focuses on ..."
- "Prior work has mainly addressed ..., leaving ... relatively understudied."
- "The limitation of this line of argument is that ..."

### Contribution Statement

- "Relative to existing research, the main value of this paper lies in ..."
- "This paper contributes by clarifying ..."
- "The contribution is not to replace prior findings, but to show that ..."

### Methods and Evidence

- "To identify this mechanism, this paper uses ..."
- "The empirical or analytical strategy is designed to distinguish ... from ..."
- "The evidence is sufficient to support the following limited claim: ..."

### Result Compression

- "The main finding can be summarized as follows."
- "More importantly, the result indicates ..."
- "This also means that the conventional view of ... needs to be qualified."

### Closing Move

- "The above discussion suggests that ..."
- "This helps reposition the debate on ..."
- "Accordingly, the remaining sections proceed as follows."

## Compression Rules

When compressing a journal-style paragraph:

- remove general background that does not change the argument
- merge adjacent sentences that perform the same logical function
- keep one strongest citation-supported point instead of three weak parallel points
- cut transition filler if the paragraph logic is already clear

Do not compress away the actual evidence or the boundary of the claim.

## Literature Review Style

For journal-style Chinese literature review writing:

- synthesize with higher compression
- reduce descriptive repetition
- emphasize dispute, omission, or positioning value
- foreground the paper's intervention earlier

Prefer stronger selectivity over broad but thin coverage.

## Methods and Data Style

When presenting methods or data in journal-style Chinese writing:

- explain what is necessary for evaluation
- keep routine setup concise
- spend more space on what is distinctive, risky, or decisive
- connect method choices directly to the research question

## Contribution Framing

In journal-style Chinese writing, contribution framing should be shorter and more pointed than thesis prose.

Good contribution framing usually:

- identifies the exact target of comparison
- states the incremental value precisely
- avoids padded lists of weak contributions

If the paper has only one real contribution, write one real contribution clearly instead of inflating it into three.

Prefer ranked emphasis: lead with the contribution most likely to matter to reviewers, then add only the supporting contributions that sharpen positioning.

## Common Failure Modes

Avoid:

- turning the introduction into a mini textbook
- listing literature without argumentative compression
- using long transition paragraphs that do no analytical work
- overstating novelty in order to sound publishable
- using generic claims about significance to compensate for weak positioning

## Revision Checklist

Before finalizing journal-style Chinese prose, check:

- can the opening reach the research problem quickly
- does each paragraph perform a distinct argumentative task
- are weak or repetitive sentences removed
- is the contribution specific rather than ceremonial
- does the prose sound publishable rather than merely complete

## Journal-Style Rewrite Heuristics

When revising draft text toward journal style:

- move the research problem earlier
- turn descriptive literature summary into argumentative positioning
- cut explanatory detours unless they are necessary for identification or interpretation
- rewrite vague contribution claims into concrete comparative claims
