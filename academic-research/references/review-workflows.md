# Review Workflows

## Use Cases

Use this reference when the task involves multiple sources and the output depends on synthesis rather than a single-paper summary.

## Comparison Matrix

Normalize each source with these fields when possible:

| Field | What to capture |
| --- | --- |
| Citation key | Short identifier such as `Smith2024` |
| Topic | Subproblem or thematic focus |
| Research question | What the paper is trying to answer |
| Method | Model, theory, design, or analytical approach |
| Data or corpus | Dataset, sample, archive, or experiment setting |
| Findings | Main result |
| Limitation | Constraint, blind spot, or threat to validity |
| Relevance | Why it matters to the user's project |

## Grouping Strategies

Choose one dominant grouping strategy before writing:

- By problem: best when different papers attack the same question from different angles.
- By method: best when the user wants to compare techniques or paradigms.
- By chronology: best when the field has clear historical phases.
- By data or population: best when evidence depends on context or sample.
- By theoretical lens: best when conceptual framing is the core distinction.

## Synthesis Moves

Use these moves to convert notes into literature review prose:

- `Convergence`: show where multiple sources align on a pattern or claim.
- `Contrast`: explain how methods, assumptions, or findings differ.
- `Qualification`: narrow a broad claim by specifying scope conditions.
- `Gap`: identify what remains untested, weakly evidenced, or methodologically constrained.
- `Bridge`: connect two clusters of literature that are usually discussed separately.

## Research-Gap Heuristics

A defensible research gap usually looks like one of these:

- A population, language, dataset, or setting is underrepresented.
- A method performs well but is not evaluated under realistic constraints.
- Findings are inconsistent because studies use incompatible metrics or assumptions.
- The field explains `what` but not `why`, or vice versa.
- A theory exists without robust empirical support.
- A practical problem matters, but current work optimizes a proxy instead.

Do not call something a research gap only because the user wants novelty. Tie the gap to what the sources do and do not establish.

## Related Work Writing Pattern

A reliable paragraph pattern is:

1. Topic sentence naming the subtheme.
2. One to three sentences summarizing the shared direction in the literature.
3. One sentence highlighting a tension, limitation, or missing angle.
4. Optional bridge sentence connecting to the next subtheme.

## Quality Check

Before finalizing a review, confirm:

- Each paragraph compares sources instead of listing them.
- Each major claim can be traced to at least one source in the materials.
- The stated gap is specific enough that a reader could test it.
- Citations are placeholders only when the exact source mapping is not yet confirmed.
